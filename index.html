Abstract

Exploring a machine learning system to generate meaningful object images from multiple textual descriptions, emulating human creativity, is a significant challenge in computer vision and machine learning as humans are able to construct a new and surprising object, but machines strive to emulate data distribution. In this paper, we develop a creative learning strategy to generate a meaningful object image that exhibits novelty, surprise and value, utilizing text concepts of different objects. Initially, we propose a neural swapping process that constructs a novel embedding by learning a neural network to swap the embeddings of text concepts. This novel embedding is then used to generate a fresh image through a generator of a diffusion model. Furthermore, we introduce an evaluation method that assesses surprise and value of the fresh image by comparing suitable CLIP distances between the fresh image and the original concepts and generations. Additionally, we utilize the evaluation method to sample the most promising swapping embedding from a set of random embeddings for training the swapping network. Our experiments focus on text pairs of objects from ImageNet, and our results demonstrate that our approach outperforms recent methods such as Stable-Diffusion2, DALLE2, ERNIE-ViLG2 and Bing in generating novel and surprising object images, even when the associated concepts appear to be implausible, such as "lionfish-abacus" and "Kangaroo-pears" (see Figs. 1 and 5)
